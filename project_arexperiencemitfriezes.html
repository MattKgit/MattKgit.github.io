AR Experience
MIT Friezes

For the final project in Mobile Operating Systems and Applications (CSCI E-76), a course taken through Harvard Extension School, I was part of a two-person team to create an AR application to provide information about historic scientists and engineers whose names were inscribed on a series of friezes along the top of buildings around MIT's central Killian Court.

(Description of functionality needed.)

Achievements

  • Help conceive of the concept, and test out the augmented reality (AR) capabilities, without the use of ARKit.
  • Create objective C iOS functionality to use iPhone location and orientation to identify which frieze was being referenced by the user.
  • Conduct research of the images of the people inscribed on the friezes, have artwork created as caricatures, both as individuals and groups. Format the completed artwork.
  • Create compass feature to orient the user and aid them in locating the friezes in Killian Court.
